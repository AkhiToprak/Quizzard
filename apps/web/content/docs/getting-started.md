Notemage is a study workspace built around three things: a notebook you can fill with text and drawings, a personal AI tutor that knows what's on your pages, and study tools (flashcards, quizzes, decks, mind maps) that you can spin up from any page.

## Create an account

Notemage uses email and password authentication.

1. Open **/auth/register**.
2. Pick a **username** (3–20 characters, letters / numbers / underscores) and an email.
3. Set a password.
4. You're in.

If you'd rather try the product first, the landing page **Get started** button takes you straight to registration.

## Sign in

1. Go to **/auth/login**.
2. Enter your email and password.

If you fail to sign in five times in a row, the account is locked for one hour. This is a brute-force protection on the auth route — wait it out, or reset your password.

## What's in your dashboard

After signing in you land on **/dashboard**. From there you can:

- Manage To-Do's.
- Open recent notebooks.
- See your XP, level, and current study streak.
- Add upcoming exams to the countdown.
- Track daily study goals.

The dashboard is the hub — everything else lives one click away.

## Your first notebook

1. From the Notebooks page, hit **New notebook**.
2. Give it a name.
3. Inside the notebook, create a **section**, then a **page**.
4. Pages can be either a **text page** (rich editor) or a **canvas page** (Excalidraw drawing surface). Pick whichever fits the material.

Once a page has content on it, the rest of Notemage opens up — you can ask your personal Mage Chat to explain it, generate flashcards from it, build a quiz, draft a presentation, or rough out a study plan.
